Order of Change

Two Forms of Change

1st Order — Evolutionary

Gradual / Adaptive

  • Limited scope, controlled rollout
  • No fundamental redesign of systems
  • Less fear and resistance
  • Easier to implement, lower risk
2nd Order — Revolutionary

Radical

  • Affects entire organization at once
  • Breaks with the past — new identity
  • High uncertainty and resistance
  • Requires strong, visible leadership

Lewin's Model

Three Psychological
Phases of Change

1
Unfreezing
Generate willingness · Question old practices · Cannot be skipped
2
Moving
Develop new behaviors · Most uncertain phase · Requires ongoing support
3
Freezing
Anchor new behaviors permanently · Without this, old habits return

Critical Success Factors

✓
Clear Leadership & Commitment
Top management must champion the change visibly
✓
Transparent Communication
Explain the "why" — regular updates throughout all phases
✓
Employee Involvement
Involve those affected early — address resistance before it compounds
✓
Realistic Planning
Achievable milestones — allow time for Unfreezing and Freezing
✓
Process-Oriented Goals
Monitor how change is managed — adjust throughout, not just at the end
