# Synthesis & Problem Definition in UX

**Type:** Lesson
**Duration:** 90–120 minutes
**Audience:** Intermediate
**Methods:** Affinity Mapping · How Might We (HMW)

---

## Key Concepts

### Synthesis
The process of moving raw data up the ladder of abstraction — turning observations into insights, and insights into a problem worth solving. Most research stops at observation. Synthesis is where direction begins.

### Affinity Mapping
A method for clustering research findings by theme until patterns emerge. One idea per sticky, clusters form naturally — you label them after, not before. Stopping at themes without naming the insight each reveals is the most common mistake.

### How Might We (HMW)
A framing technique that turns an insight into a design-ready problem statement. A good HMW hits the Goldilocks zone: specific enough to act on, open enough to be answered five completely different ways.

### The Ladder of Abstraction
The mental model that connects data → observation → insight → problem statement. Each rung requires deliberate interpretation. "What happened" lives at the bottom. "What it means for design" lives at the top.

## Practice Activities

### Affinity Mapping Practice
Groups of 2–3 work through a shared dataset of research findings. Each finding goes on a separate sticky, groups cluster silently, then label themes and write one insight statement per cluster. Ends with a cross-group share-back.

### HMW Writing
Each student takes their strongest insight from the affinity mapping exercise and writes three HMW statements ranging from too narrow to too broad. Pairs pick the one that hits the Goldilocks zone and explain their reasoning.
