## Slide 2: Why Change Management Matters
- **Main Message:** Change is both necessary and difficult
- **Key Stat:** >60% of change projects fail to fully achieve their goals
- **Why It Matters:**
  - Increasing speed of organizational change
  - Growing pressure to adapt
  - Need for structured approach
- **Visual:** Statistics/trend arrow

---

## Slide 3: Three Levels of Change
- **Change (Small-Medium Scale)**
  - Department-specific projects
  - Contained impact
  - Example: Analytics tool adoption
  
- **Conversion (Cross-Departmental)**
  - Visible effects across departments
  - No fundamental strategic rethink
  - Example: Customer service workflow redesign
  
- **Transformation (Full Strategic Realignment)**
  - Entire organization affected
  - Shift in business fields/identity
  - Example: Preussag/TUI (steel → tourism)

---

## Slide 4: What is Change Management?
- **Definition (Vahs & Weiand):**
  - *"Preparation, analysis, planning, implementation, evaluation and continuous further development of integrated change measures"*
  
- **Rank & Scheinpflug's View:**
  - Permanent, planned procedure (not one-time)
  - Requires two dimensions:
    - **Effectiveness:** Achieving goals + employee acceptance
    - **Efficiency:** Time and cost optimization

---

## Slide 5: Two Forms of Change
- **1st Order - Evolutionary (Gradual/Adaptive)**
  - Limited scope
  - No fundamental redesign
  - Less fear and resistance
  - Easier to implement
  
- **2nd Order - Revolutionary (Radical)**
  - Affects entire organization
  - Breaks with the past
  - High uncertainty and resistance
  - Requires strong leadership

---

## Slide 6: Vahs & Weiand's Layer Model
**Four Depth Levels of Change:**

| Level | Type | Description | Effort |
|-------|------|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **Restructuring** | Optimize existing processes & structures | Low |
| 2 | **Reorientation** | Strategic realignment, redesign business fields | Medium |
| 3 | **Revitalisation** | Change competencies & employee behavior | High |
| 4 | **Remodeling** | Change core values & culture | Very High |

---

## Slide 7: Types of Change Goals
- **Material Goals**
  - *Monetary:* Profit growth, cost reduction, profitability
  - *Non-monetary:* Customer numbers, quality, service delivery
  
- **Behavioral Goals**
  - *Internal:* Corporate culture, mindset, new competencies
  - *External:* Customer/supplier relations, public perception
  
- **Overarching Strategic Goal:**
  - Increase competitiveness and survival capacity

---

## Slide 8: Lewin's Phase Model
**Three Psychological Phases Every Change Must Navigate:**

### Phase 1: Unfreezing
- Generate willingness to change
- Question old practices and assumptions
- Create awareness of need for change
- *Critical:* Cannot be skipped

### Phase 2: Moving
- Develop new behaviors and thinking
- People work with new approach
- Most uncertain phase
- Requires ongoing support and training

### Phase 3: Freezing (Stabilization)
- Anchor new behaviors permanently
- Without this: old habits return
- Reinforce through feedback and monitoring
- Establish new equilibrium

---

## Slide 9: Implementation Framework (Vahs)
**Material-Level Phases Building on Lewin:**

1. **Preparation/Basics** - Foundational groundwork
2. **Analysis** - Current state assessment
3. **Planning** - Strategy and roadmap
4. **Implementation** - Execution
5. **Control & Further Development** - Monitoring and refinement

*Cycle repeats:* Change management is continuous, not linear

---

## Slide 10: Success Rates - Reality Check
**Study Results (Across 2006-2010):**

| Study | Key Finding |
|-------|-------------|
| **Capgemini (2010)** | 66% avg achievement; only 1 in 8 fully succeeded; 1 in 4 was a flop |
| **IBM "Making Change Work" (2007)** | <40% met targets fully; 16% failed entirely |
| **C4 Consulting (2006)** | <20% complete success; >1 in 3 deemed failed or barely successful |

**Why the gap?**
- Lack of management commitment
- Unclear goals and vision
- Poor communication
- Failure to involve employees
- Unrealistic planning

---

## Slide 11: Four Fields of Action (Vahs & Weiand)
**Integrated Approach - All Must Align:**

- **Strategy** → Develop competitive advantages (reactive or proactive)
- **Corporate Culture** → Values, standards, attitudes that anchor change in behavior
- **Technology** → Production methods, tools, IT systems
- **Organisation** → Processes, structures, cost efficiency, throughput

*Key Insight:* Changes in one field ripple to others. All must be coordinated for optimal outcome.

---

## Slide 12: Critical Success Factors
**To Increase Chances of Success:**

✓ **Clear Leadership & Commitment**
- Top management must champion the change
- Visible support from executives

✓ **Transparent Communication**
- Explain the "why" and expected outcomes
- Regular updates throughout all phases

✓ **Employee Involvement**
- Involve those affected early
- Address concerns and resistance

✓ **Realistic Planning**
- Set achievable milestones
- Allow adequate time for Unfreezing and Freezing

✓ **Process-Oriented Goals**
- Monitor how the change is being managed
- Adjust as needed, not just outcomes

---

## Slide 13: Key Takeaways
- **Change is inevitable** but challenging (>60% projects struggle)
- **Systematic approach required:** definition → planning → implementation → stabilization
- **Psychological dimension is critical:** Lewin's three phases cannot be skipped
- **Multiple dimensions matter:** strategy, culture, technology, organization must align
- **Success requires:** clear goals, committed leadership, employee buy-in, realistic timelines
- **Change management is ongoing**, not a one-time project
